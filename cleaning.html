Pro-Style Cleaning Cloths

Exactly what to buy for kitchen, bathroom, glass, and appliances — with colour-coding, care, and a weekly setup.

Typeface: Helvetica. Tables use rounded corners and alternate rows for quick scanning.

Colour Coding (to avoid cross‑contamination)

Blue → Glass/Mirrors
Green → Kitchen (worktops, appliances)
Red → Bathroom/Toilet
Yellow → General/Dusting

Keep colours consistent. Wash reds separately if used on toilets.

What to Buy (by cloth type)

Cloth Type Spec to Look For Best For Avoid/Notes
General Microfibre ~300–350 GSM, tight weave, stitched edge Worktops, cabinets, appliances exteriors Avoid fabric softener (kills absorbency)
Glass & Polishing Microfibre Low‑pile, lint‑free, waffle or smooth weave Windows, mirrors, stainless steel buffing Use dry or slightly damp for streak‑free
Scrub Microfibre / Non‑scratch Pad Microfibre one side, scrub mesh the other Hobs, baked‑on spills, sink marks Test first on delicate coatings
Melamine “Magic Eraser” Dense melamine foam blocks Scuffs on walls, stubborn marks Abrasive — avoid glossy/soft plastics
Drying Towel (Waffle/Plush) 400–600 GSM waffle or plush Drying glass, shower screens, sinks Wring out frequently for best results
Microfibre Mop Pads Flat mop pads, hook‑and‑loop Floors (kitchen, bathroom, hallways) Keep separate pads for kitchen vs bathroom

Care & Washing (to make them last)

Do Don’t Why
Wash at 40–60°C with liquid detergent No fabric softener or dryer sheets Softener coats fibres → poor absorbency
Air‑dry or low heat tumble High heat tumble on plush cloths High heat can melt/smash fibres
Wash reds separately Mix toilet cloths with others Hygiene & staining prevention
Pre‑rinse greasy/oily cloths Let oils build up between washes Maintains cleaning performance

Quick Buy List (one‑time setup)

  • 8–10 × colour‑coded general microfibres (blue/green/red/yellow)
  • 2 × glass & polishing microfibres
  • 1–2 × non‑scratch scrub pads
  • 1 × drying towel (waffle or plush)
  • 2 × microfibre mop pads (kitchen + bathroom)
  • Optional: melamine blocks for scuffs

Tip: Buy multi‑packs to save money; look for stitched edges and GSM listed on the label.
