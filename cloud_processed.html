Cloud Computing in Indian Retail: Current Landscape, Challenges, and Opportunities (2025)

Prepared by: Solution Architect Date: 03 Sep 2025 Audience: Retail & Travel Technology Leaders

Short answer: Cloud is now a core enabler of Indian retail's omnichannel, analytics, and AI ambitions, underpinned by rapid eretail growth, ubiquitous UPI payments, and expanding local cloud regions that satisfy dataresidency demands. Over 20232028, India's public cloud market is projected to grow at more than 24% CAGR, while eretail GMV is set for doubledigit growth toward 2030creating both cost and innovation advantages for retailers who tackle compliance, connectivity, and FinOps challenges proactively.

Overview

India's eretail market reached roughly $60 billion GMV in 2024 and is projected to grow to $170$190 billion by 2030 as nearly 1 in 10 retail dollars moves online, making cloud-native scale and agility essential for retailers of all sizes.

Public cloud adoption is accelerating: IDC forecasts India's public cloud services market will reach $25.5B by 2028 (CAGR 24.3% for 202328), reflecting modernization, multicloud strategies, and GenAI workloads across retail value chains.

Payments infrastructure is a key tailwind: UPI crossed 20.01 billion transactions in August 2025 (24.85 lakh crore), normalizing digital purchasing behaviors that rely on reliable, lowlatency cloud backends at national scale.

Fact nuggets
  • India became the world's secondlargest online shopper base in 2024 with 270M+ shoppers; eretail growth is expected to reaccelerate from late2025.
  • UPI averaged ~645 million transactions daily in Aug2025, underscoring alwayson, clouddependent retail payment flows.
  • ONDC surpassed 200M cumulative transactions by March2025 across mobility, retail, and logistics, widening the addressable digital commerce surface for cloudconnected sellers.

Mechanics

Omnichannel core: Retailers deploy microservices on public cloud for catalog, pricing, inventory, checkout, and returns, elastically scaling during peak events while keeping latency low via India regions.

Data platforms: Unified lakes and warehouses centralize clickstream, POS, ERP, and logistics data to power nearrealtime dashboards and ML for demand forecasting, replenishment, and fraud detection.

  • Flipkart's multiyear collaboration uses Google Cloud infrastructure, analytics, and ML to handle peak traffic and personalize experiences across Tier2/3 markets.
  • Myntra integrates Vertex AI/Imagen to deliver generative AI product inspiration experiences, demonstrating cloudhosted AI in hightraffic retail journeys.
  • Nykaa's AWSbased data platform accelerated refreshes 48 and reduced software costs ~30% during PoC, enabling faster insights at scale.

Edge + store tech: Store gateways, cameras, and sensors stream to cloud for computer vision lossprevention and queue analytics, while regional cloud zones support DR and lowlatency services incountry.

Key metrics

Measure what matters: Cloud unlocks granular observability; the following KPIs help align engineering, operations, and merchandising outcomes in Indian retail contexts.

KPI Definition Why it matters
Order fill rate Share of orders fulfilled in full and on time; ties inventory accuracy to promise reliability. Directly impacts NPS and repeat purchase; underpins omnichannel trust.
Stockout rate Percent of demand lost due to zero available stock across online and stores. Cloud forecasting and realtime inventory reduce lost sales and basket abandonment.
Inventory turnover COGS average inventory; rotation speed across categories and nodes. Optimizes working capital; cloud analytics spot slow movers and excess.
Conversion rate Sessions to orders on eretail channels, segmented by traffic source and device. Reflects productmarket fit and experience; personalization/AI can lift outcomes.
AOV Average order value; monitored with promotions and crosssell experiments. Improves unit economics; informs pricing and bundling strategies.
Cloud cost / order Total cloud spend divided by orders; tracked by service and environment. Core FinOps metric to prevent overprovisioning; targets sustainable scale.
KPI Definition Why it matters
p95 checkout latency 95th percentile latency for key APIs (auth, cart, pay) during peaks. Correlates to conversion and dropoff; guides caching and capacity plans.
Data freshness Lag between source events and analytical availability (e.g., minutes). Timely insights drive replenishment and pricing agility; Nykaa improved refreshes 48.
Forecast MAPE Mean Absolute Percentage Error across SKUs for demand forecasts. Lower errors reduce stockouts and markdowns; cloud ML accelerates iteration.
Payment success rate Authorized and captured payments payment attempts across UPI/cards. Reliability on UPIheavy traffic; sensitive to PSP/API latency and retries.
Compliance SLA Adherence to DPDP, CERTIn (logs, incident reporting), and RBI localization. Avoids penalties and disruption; designs must embed controls by default.
Uptime (SLA) Service availability for critical journeys (browse, search, checkout). Singles' Day/Big Billion Days scale depends on wellarchitected reliability.

Case study: Nykaa unified data platform on AWS

Nykaa operates an omnichannel beauty and fashion business serving 40M+ monthly active users with 165+ stores and 6,000+ brands, requiring a scalable, governed data foundation.

By consolidating into an AWSbased lake and unified data platform, Nykaa cut core software expenses by ~30% during PoC and increased daily data refreshes 48, enabling faster reporting and analytics.

  • Standardized ingestion, governance, and query layers for "One Nykaa, One Data," streamlining insight delivery from weeks to hours.
  • Cloud elasticity supports peak season surges and experimentation cycles in merchandising and marketing with faster feedback loops.

Data analytics and AI in practice

Retailers centralize clickstream, POS, OMS/WMS, and payments telemetry to power nearrealtime decisions; cloudnative pipelines reduce latency between signal and action for assortment, pricing, and availability.

UPI's scale normalizes instant, lowcost payments nationwide, demanding resilient payment orchestration and reconciliation pipelines built on robust cloud primitives and observability.

Generative AI is live in production: Myntra's Vertex AI/Imagenbased feature converts prompts into dcor inspirations, evidencing customerfacing AI at retail traffic levels when backed by managed cloud services.

Compliance is designtime: DPDP Act mandates lawful processing, safeguards, and penalties up to INR 250 crore for certain failures, while CERTIn directions require 180day log retention in India and sixhour incident reporting.

Current scenario

Cloud usage is broadbased across leaders such as Flipkart, Myntra, and Nykaa for elasticity, analytics, and AI, with local regions from AWS and Google enabling incountry lowlatency deployments and DR.

Macro drivers include the spread of digital commerce beyond metros, UPI ubiquity, and rising ONDC participation, which together expand digital demand surfaces and drive APIfirst, eventdriven architectures.

Challenges

  • Compliance and data protection: DPDP obligations, CERTIn log retention/reporting, and RBI data localization for payment system data shape architecture and vendor choices.
  • Cost governance (FinOps): Rapid scale and GenAI workloads can inflate spend without uniteconomics guardrails like cost per order and perservice budgets tied to outcomes.
  • Latency and resiliency: Peak events require p95/p99 discipline across app, data, and network; multiAZ/region in India strengthens DR while meeting dataresidency.
  • Integration complexity: Legacy POS/ERP and marketplace/ONDC integrations demand robust data contracts and observability to prevent brittle, expensive interfaces.

Opportunities

  • GenAI for content, search, and inspiration: Proven by Myntra, retailers can scale AI assistants and creative tooling where managed cloud AI reduces timetovalue.
  • Retail media and personalization: Firstparty data lakes can power insession offers and media monetization with strict governance under DPDP.
  • ONDC channel strategy: Joining as buyer/seller/logistics participants extends reach into new categories and geographies with interoperable cloud connectors.
  • Resilient incountry deployments: Dual AWS regions and two GCP regions support activeactive or warmstandby DR within India for regulated workloads.

Cloud governance and compliance KPIs

KPI Definition and target intent
Log retention adherence Percent of systems retaining required logs in India for 180 days and retrievable within SLA to meet CERTIn directions.
Incident reporting SLA Mean time to notify CERTIn within 6 hours for applicable incidents, including standardized templates and runbooks.
DPDP control coverage Share of systems assessed for lawful basis, consent, minimization, retention, and breach response with evidence trails.
Payment data localization 100% storage of payment system data within India with deletion outside India within stipulated timeframes where applicable.
